## Page 4 — Why borrowers are declined (or delayed)

Borrowers are often told “not yet” for reasons that are explainable—and workable—with time and structure:

| Pattern                       | What it often means for readiness work       |
| ----------------------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| High utilization              | Revolving balances crowding available credit |
| Recent inquiries / new credit | Timing and layering risk                     |
| Collections / charge-offs     | Documentation + dispute/settlement paths     |
| Thin or young credit          | Education + responsible tradeline strategy   |
| Identity / mixed-file issues  | Verification and correction workflows        |
| Documentation gaps            | Income, housing, or identity packet gaps     |
| Derogatories still reporting  | Staff-mediated dispute support where fit     |

**Important:** LRP does not decide eligibility. Lenders and underwriters do. We help borrowers and partners understand the work ahead.

## Page 5 — How our process works

1. **Refer** — LO or realtor sends a near-miss borrower via form or portal
2. **Intake** — LRP reviews completeness; accepts or requests missing items
3. **Plan** — Borrower receives education + prioritized tasks
4. **Work** — Staff-mediated dispute and documentation support where required
5. **Update** — Partner receives scoped progress (digest / portal)
6. **Signal** — Advisory Lending Readiness Score™ / band when appropriate
7. **Return** — Borrower resumes the financing conversation with their LO

**Principle:** Clarity over hype. Dignity over shame.

## Page 6 — Mortgage score education (advisory framing)

Partners and borrowers often say “mortgage score” when they mean the credit picture a lender will review.

**How we teach it:**

- Credit is multi-bureau and multi-factor—not a single magic number
- Utilization, payment history, derogatories, and inquiries all matter
- Mortgage underwriting uses overlays and product rules beyond a score
- Our **Lending Readiness Score™** is an **advisory** organizing tool—not a CRA score and not an underwriting decision

**Always pair education with the long disclaimer** on this page.

## Page 10–11 — Case studies (fictional composites)

> These scenarios are **fictional composites** for training. They are not real client testimonials and do not include fabricated before/after FICO figures.

### Scenario A — High utilization, strong income

**Situation:** Conventional purchase; borrower motivated; revolving utilization elevated.  
**LRP work:** Education on utilization mechanics; payment prioritization tasks; partner weekly digest.  
**Partner value:** LO keeps the relationship; borrower has a concrete plan instead of “call me later.”  
**Outcome framing:** Borrower returns for a financing conversation when prepared—**no approval claim**.

### Scenario B — Collection + documentation gaps

**Situation:** FHA exploratory; collection reporting; missing ID packet items.  
**LRP work:** Document checklist; staff-mediated dispute path where appropriate; realtor kept in the loop on stages (not underwriting).  
**Partner value:** Shared vocabulary; fewer shame-based conversations.  
**Outcome framing:** Process clarity; eligibility still decided by lender.

### Scenario C — Identity / mixed-file concern

**Situation:** Suspicious personal info variance across bureaus.  
**LRP work:** Verification workflow; education; escalate carefully; no legal conclusions in marketing.  
**Partner value:** Risk of wasted applications reduced through preparation.  
**Outcome framing:** Advisory readiness only.

## Page 12 — Frequently asked questions

**How long will this take?**  
It depends on the file. We set priorities and update you on a defined cadence. We do not guarantee dates.

**How much does it cost?**  
Partnership and consumer engagement terms are reviewed in the briefing. We do not pressure borrowers with miracle pricing claims.

**Can you guarantee results?**  
No. We do not guarantee score changes, approvals, or funding.

**Will this delay my closing?**  
If a borrower is not yet ready, rushing an application can create more harm. Our job is preparation—not forcing a calendar.

**Do you replace my processor / underwriter?**  
No. We support readiness. Your team underwrites.

**What do partners see?**  
Scoped status, progress digests, and (where enabled) portal visibility—not a cross-tenant marketplace.
