# Chapter 1 — Understanding credit

Credit is a record of how you have borrowed and repaid money, as reported by companies to consumer reporting agencies (often called credit bureaus). Mortgage lenders typically review more than one bureau and more than one factor.

## Building blocks

| Factor (plain language)      | Why it matters for mortgages                    |
| ---------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| Payment history              | Missed payments can weigh heavily               |
| Amounts owed / utilization   | How much revolving credit you are using         |
| Length of history            | Younger or thin files can be harder to evaluate |
| Mix of credit                | Different account types may appear              |
| New credit / inquiries       | Recent applications can affect timing           |
| Public records / collections | Derogatory items may require a plan             |

Exact scoring formulas are proprietary. Treat “point impact” charts on social media as entertainment, not advice.

## Reports vs scores

- A **credit report** is the detailed file
- A **credit score** is a number derived from a model
- Your **Lending Readiness Score™** (if you have one with LRP) is an **advisory** organizer—not a bureau score
