# Chapter 4 — Charge-offs

A charge-off typically means a creditor wrote off a debt as a loss for accounting purposes. The debt may still be owed, and the item may still report.

## Key ideas

- Charge-off ≠ automatic “gone forever”
- Documentation matters (agreements, balances, who owns the debt now)
- Your plan may include verification, negotiation pathways, or dispute paths—guided by staff when enrolled
- Never rely on social media “delete charge-off overnight” promises

Bring: account numbers (safely), letters, settlement offers, and questions. Your LO still decides lending—not LRP.
